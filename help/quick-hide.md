# Quick-Hide Feature

> **[Open this page in Quilltap](/characters)**

The quick-hide feature is a powerful organizational tool that lets you temporarily hide content tagged with specific tags. It helps you focus on your current work by reducing clutter and showing only what's relevant.

## What is Quick-Hide?

Quick-hide lets you toggle the visibility of characters and content based on their tags. When you hide a tag, all content tagged with that tag disappears from your view across the application. When you show the tag again, everything reappears. No data is ever deleted—it's just temporarily hidden.

## Accessing Quick-Hide

### Location

The quick-hide control is located in the **sidebar footer** (bottom left of your screen):

- Look for the **eye icon** with crossed lines (closed eye) or an open eye
- Click this icon to open the quick-hide menu

### In the Sidebar

When you have hidden tags, the icon will show a **closed eye**, indicating that something is currently hidden. Click to open the menu and see what's hidden.

When nothing is hidden, the icon shows an **open eye**, indicating everything is visible.

## Using Quick-Hide

### Opening the Menu

1. Click the **quick-hide icon** in the sidebar footer
2. A dropdown menu appears showing all available quick-hide tags
3. Each tag has a visibility toggle (eye icon) next to it

### Hiding a Tag

1. Open the quick-hide menu
2. Find the tag you want to hide
3. Click the tag or its eye icon to hide it
4. The eye changes to a closed eye to show it's hidden
5. Content with that tag immediately disappears from your view

### Showing a Tag

1. Open the quick-hide menu
2. Find the tag you want to show (it will have a closed eye icon)
3. Click to show it
4. The eye changes to an open eye to show it's visible
5. Content with that tag reappears

### Multiple Hidden Tags

You can hide multiple tags at once:

- Each tag's visibility is toggled independently
- Hide as many or as few tags as you need
- The quick-hide icon shows if any tags are currently hidden

## What Gets Hidden

When you hide a tag, it affects visibility in several places:

### Character Sidebar

- Characters tagged with hidden tags won't appear in the sidebar
- Favorites with hidden tags are filtered out
- Your character list becomes shorter and more focused

### Chat Sidebar

- Chats involving only hidden-tag characters don't appear
- Chats with a mix of hidden and visible characters still appear
- This keeps important ongoing conversations visible

### Homepage

- Favorite characters with hidden tags are hidden from the "Favorite Characters" section
- Recent chats involving only hidden-tag characters are filtered
- The homepage shows only your active, visible content

### Quick Actions

- When suggesting characters for new chats, hidden-tag characters are filtered out
- Connection profile selection lists are not affected by quick-hide
- Image profile selection lists are not affected by quick-hide

## Setting Up Quick-Hide

Before you can use quick-hide, tags must be marked as "quick-hide enabled" in your Settings.

### Enabling Quick-Hide for Tags

1. Go to **Settings** → **Tags** tab
2. Find the tag you want to use for quick-hide
3. Click to customize the tag
4. Look for the **Quick Hide** toggle at the bottom
5. Enable it to make this tag available for quick-hide
6. The tag now appears in your quick-hide menu

### Only Quick-Hide Enabled Tags Appear

The quick-hide menu only shows tags that you've explicitly marked as quick-hide enabled in Settings. This keeps the menu organized and focused on tags you actually want to use for hiding.

## Use Cases

### Use Case 1: Manage Multiple Projects

- Create tags for each project (e.g., "project-a", "project-b")
- When working on project-a, hide characters tagged with project-b
- Quickly switch between projects by toggling tags

### Use Case 2: Genre-Based Organization

- Create tags for different genres (e.g., "fantasy", "sci-fi", "romance")
- When working on a fantasy novel, hide sci-fi and romance characters
- Focus on only the characters relevant to your current creative project

### Use Case 3: Archive Management

- Create an "archived" or "inactive" tag
- Tag characters you're not actively using
- Hide them to keep your active character list clean
- Unhide anytime to retrieve archived characters

### Use Case 4: Active Development

- Create a "wip" (work-in-progress) tag
- Tag characters actively in development
- Hide all other characters to focus on your current characters
- See only what you're currently working on

### Use Case 5: Frequency-Based Filtering

- Create tags like "daily", "weekly", "occasional"
- When you have limited time, show only "daily" characters
- Focus your energy on characters you interact with most

## Quick-Hide vs. Other Features

### Quick-Hide vs. Character Deletion

- **Quick-hide** — Temporary, reversible, data stays intact
- **Deletion** — Permanent, removes data, cannot be undone
- Use quick-hide to temporarily reduce clutter
- Use deletion only when you're sure you want to remove content

### Quick-Hide vs. Search

- **Search** — Find specific content by name or keyword
- **Quick-hide** — Hides entire categories of content at once
- Use search when looking for one specific item
- Use quick-hide to focus on broad categories

### Quick-Hide vs. Favorite System

- **Favorites** — Mark content you use frequently
- **Quick-hide** — Hide content you don't want to see
- Both can be used together for powerful organization

## Tips for Effective Quick-Hide

1. **Create high-level tags** — Make quick-hide tags represent large categories
   - Example: "archived" instead of individual project names
   - Helps you hide multiple characters with one toggle

2. **Use meaningful names** — Quick-hide tag names should be clear and easy to understand
   - Example: "inactive" is clearer than "x"
   - You'll remember what they mean even if you haven't used quick-hide in a while

3. **Don't overuse** — Avoid creating too many quick-hide tags
   - 5-10 tags is usually enough
   - Too many tags makes the menu overwhelming
   - Consolidate related tags instead of creating many similar ones

4. **Combine with visual styles** — Use distinct colors and emojis for quick-hide tags
   - Makes them easier to identify in the quick-hide menu
   - Helps you quickly remember which tag to toggle
   - See the Tags help file for customization details

5. **Review regularly** — Check your quick-hide setup periodically
   - Are you hiding tags that are no longer needed?
   - Are there new tags you want to enable for quick-hide?
   - Adjust your setup as your projects evolve

## Workflow Example

Here's a typical quick-hide workflow:

1. **Setup** — Enable "archived" tag as quick-hide in Settings
2. **Organize** — Tag inactive characters as "archived"
3. **Hide** — Click quick-hide icon, hide the "archived" tag
4. **Focus** — See only active characters in your sidebar
5. **Retrieve** — When you want to use an archived character, toggle the tag to show it
6. **Resume** — Hide again to focus on your active work

## Keyboard Shortcuts

Currently, quick-hide is accessed through the sidebar icon. Keyboard shortcuts may be added in future updates for even faster access.

## Saving Your Preferences

Your quick-hide settings are automatically saved:

- Which tags you have hidden/shown
- Your quick-hide preferences persist when you log out
- Each user has their own quick-hide settings
- Share your workspace with others without affecting their quick-hide preferences

The quick-hide feature is essential for managing large character rosters and complex projects. Use it to keep your workspace organized and focused!
