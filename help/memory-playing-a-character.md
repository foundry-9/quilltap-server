# When You Take the Reins Yourself

> **[Open this page in Quilltap](/settings?tab=memory)**

It is one of the quieter pleasures of this establishment that you need not always leave a character to the machine's devices. Now and again you may wish to *be* them for a spell — to take up the pen, speak in their voice, and play the part with your own hand. Quilltap has long allowed this courtesy. What it did not, until lately, allow was for the character to **remember** any of it.

Picture the awkwardness. You spend a long evening playing a duelist — you draw their blade, you refuse the captain's bribe, you confess, in the first person, a fondness you had sworn to keep secret. Then you hand the duelist back to the machine, and the machine, taking up where you left off, hasn't the faintest notion that any of it occurred. The vow goes unremembered; the confession evaporates; the character wakes, as it were, with no recollection of the very scene you just lived through together. It was as though the understudy returned to find the leading player had been struck with amnesia in the wings.

That door is now closed.

## What Now Happens

When you play a character directly, that character keeps the same Commonplace Book any of its machine-driven fellows would. From the turns you drove it, it now forms its own memories of two kinds:

- **Of itself** — the decisions it made, the lines it would not cross, the small revelations that surfaced while you spoke for it. When you write "I told her I would not go," the Book understands that the *I* is the character, and files the resolve under the character's own name.
- **Of the company it kept** — its impressions of the others in the scene, formed exactly as it would form them were the machine at the helm. The duelist remembers the captain's threat; the confidante remembers who confided.

So when control passes back to the machine, the character carries forward everything it built up while you were at the reins — the impressions, the facts, the freshly-knit relationships — and picks up the thread as though it had been paying attention all along. Which, in a manner of speaking, it now has.

## A Few Particulars, For the Curious

- **No switch to throw.** This asks nothing of you and offers no dial to turn. The moment you play a character, it begins remembering. There is no setting because there is no decision to make.
- **The same gentle limits apply.** A character you play is held to precisely the same hourly ration of memories as any other, so a marathon role-play does not flood its shelves. The Book remains a Book, not a warehouse.
- **It tells the others, too.** Just as before, the *other* characters in the scene continue to form their own memories *about* the figure you are playing. That was always so; what is new is that the figure now remembers them back.
- **A note on revisiting the past.** Should you ever ask the Book to [reconsider a conversation already had](memory-regenerate.md), it will now offer up the memories your played character would have formed as well — there for your inspection before anything is written down.
